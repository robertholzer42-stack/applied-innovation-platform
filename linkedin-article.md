# I Open-Sourced My AI Innovation Practice. Here's Why.

Most organizations innovate but often not as effectively as they could.

They run ideaion and design thinking workshops but may know who or what they are innovating for. They do signal monitoring and scenario planning but don't fully understand the customer needs or tie it to business goals and objectives. They analyze market trends but don't map the dependencies that will make or break execution.

One-dimensional innovation produces ideas that look great on a whiteboard but don't scale in your markets.

I spent the last several months thinking differently: connecting my knowledge leading innovation teams with resources from IFTF and thought provoking articles from leader's like Braden Kelley I developed an AI-powered platform that forces multi-dimensional thinking every time. It applies Future Thinking, Design Thinking, and Systems Thinking together through 13 specialized AI agents, each with its own personality, analytical framework, and output format.

Today I'm making it available to everyone.

## What the Platform Does

The Applied Innovation Platform runs inside Claude (Anthropic's AI). No infrastructure to set up, no API keys, no code to deploy. You clone a repo, upload some Markdown files, and you have a multi-dimensional innovation engine.

The 13 agents are organized into five tiers:

**Core Thinking** agents apply the three foundational lenses independently. Scout handles strategic foresight using IFTF-aligned methodology. Empathy runs human-centered design analysis. Architect maps system dependencies and feedback loops.

**Intersection** agents work where the lenses overlap, and that's where the real insights live. Visionary combines future trends with human needs to design for tomorrow's users. Integrator connects design solutions to system realities so they actually scale. Sentinel stress-tests strategies against multiple futures to find what breaks.

**Operational** agents ground everything in competitive reality, portfolio fit, measurable scoring, and change readiness.

And the **Conductor** orchestrates it all, routing challenges to the right agents, synthesizing across perspectives, and surfacing conflicts instead of hiding them.

Every opportunity gets scored on four dimensions: Desirability, Viability, Feasibility, and Adaptability (DVFA). The result is an Innovation Score that goes beyond the typical "do people want it and can we build it" test.

## Why Open Source?

I built this for my own innovation consulting practice. But the more I used it, the more I realized the architecture pattern itself is the contribution.

The idea that you can give an AI distinct analytical personalities, route problems to the right combination, and force integration across different thinking disciplines - that's a pattern anyone can adapt. Whether you're an innovation consultant, a strategy team inside a Fortune 500, or a solo founder trying to think more rigorously about your next move.

The agent skills are just Markdown files. You can read them, understand them, modify them. Add your industry's regulatory context to the Architect agent. Plug your own foresight methodology into Scout. Create entirely new agents for dimensions I haven't thought of.

## How to Get Started

1. Clone the repo from GitHub: [link to your repo]
2. Create a Claude Project and upload the skill files
3. Say: "Run a full applied innovation analysis on [your challenge]"

That's it. The setup guide walks you through alternative deployment options (Claude Code, Claude Cowork) and includes a sample engagement you can run to see the full pipeline in action.
